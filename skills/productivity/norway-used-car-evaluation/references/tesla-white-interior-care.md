# Tesla white interior care — Model 3/Y (2020+)

## Material facts

Tesla's "vegan leather" is a **PU-coated synthetic material**, not real leather. This is actually an advantage for durability:

| Property | Fact |
|:---------|:-----|
| Material | Polyurethane (PU) on polyester backing |
| Porosity | Non-porous — liquids sit on top, don't soak in |
| Real leather? | No — "vegan leather" / "premium synthetic seating material" |
| Durability | High — designed to outlast the car |
| Stain resistance | Good — most stains wipe off with damp cloth |

## Common stain sources

| Source | Risk | Easily removable? |
|:-------|:----:|:-----------------:|
| Blue jeans (denim) | 🟡 Friction dye transfer | ✅ Baby wipes / mild cleaner |
| Dark trousers | 🟡 Same as jeans | ✅ | 
| Sunscreen / lotion | 🟡 Greasy residue | ✅ Diluted APC or mild soap |
| Food / coffee | 🟢 Low risk on PU | ✅ Wipe immediately |
| Kids' shoes / car seats | 🟢 Scuff marks | ✅ Eraser sponge (melaminsvamp) |

**Denim transfer is the #1 complaint.** It looks worse than it is — it's surface dye, not absorption, and cleans off easily.

## Protection options

### 1. Weekly maintenance (cheapest, good enough)

- **Baby wipes** (e.g. WaterWipes) — wipe seats weekly, removes denim transfer and dust
- **Microfiber cloth + water** — for light cleaning
- **Cost**: ~50-100 kr/year

### 2. Ceramic coating (~500 kr, great protection)

Products confirmed safe for Tesla vegan leather:

| Product | Notes |
|:--------|:------|
| **Gyeon LeatherShield EVO** | SiO2 ceramic coating. Amazon says: *"Safe for all modern OEM-coated, synthetic, and perforated leathers, including vegan leather like Tesla's interiors."* Used by many Tesla owners with good results. |
| **303 Automotive Protectant** | UV protectant, matte finish, no colour change. Safer option than ceramic for nervous owners. |
| **Chemical Guys Silk Shine** | Cleans + protects. |

**⚠️ Gyeon warning**: A minority report discolouration on Tesla seats. Common causes:
- Insufficient pre-cleaning (dirt trapped under coating)
- Too much product (a thin layer is enough)
- Improper application (not buffed evenly)

**Safe approach**: Test on a hidden area (under seat, lower side trim) before doing the full interior.

### 3. Seat covers (full protection, ~1.500-4.000 kr)

Seat covers let you effectively "change" interior colour (e.g. white → black) without permanent modification.

## Recommendation workflow for buyers concerned about white interior

1. **Start with ceramic coating** (Gyeon or 303) + weekly baby wipes
2. **If still worried** after a month → buy seat covers
3. **Don't rush to swap to black interior** — white is a selling point for resale (was a more expensive option)
4. **Never dye/paint the seats** — ruins resale value and the material doesn't absorb dye evenly
