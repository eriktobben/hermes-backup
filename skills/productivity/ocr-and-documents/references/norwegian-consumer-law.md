# Norwegian Consumer Law — Reklamasjon (Forbrukerkjøpsloven §27)

## Core Statute

**Forbrukerkjøpsloven §27** (lovdata.no/lov/2002-06-21-34):

> *"Reklamasjon må senest skje to år etter at tingen ble levert. Dersom tingen eller deler av den ved vanlig bruk er ment å vare vesentlig lenger, er fristen for å reklamere fem år."*

Translation: Complaint must be made within 2 years of delivery. If the item or parts of it, through normal use, are meant to last significantly longer, the deadline is 5 years.

## Key Arguments for 5-Year Period on Integrated Batteries

When a product has a **non-replaceable (integrated) battery** and the battery fails:

1. **Battery is an integral part** — cannot be separated from the product. When the battery fails, the entire product is unusable.
2. **Lovens ordlyd**: "tingen **eller deler av den**" — if the part (battery) can't be replaced independently, the product's total lifespan governs the reclamation period.
3. **Forventet levetid**: A premium sports watch (Polar Vantage V2, ~3500-4500 NOK) should reasonably last >3.5 years. Lithium-ion batteries in premium devices handle 500-800 charge cycles.
4. **Bevisbyrde**: The seller must prove the expected lifespan is ≤2 years, not the consumer the opposite.

## Counterargument to "Batteri har 2 års reklamasjon"

The seller may claim a distinction between hardware/software (5 years) and battery (2 years). **Rebuttal:**
- No statutory basis exists for this distinction
- If the battery is non-replaceable, the distinction is artificial — the product fails as a whole
- Forbrukerklageutvalget has ruled in multiple cases that integrated batteries in premium electronics fall under the 5-year rule

## Escalation Path (Norway)

1. **Skriftlig krav til selger** — henvis til §27, krev retting/omlevering
2. **Forbrukerrådet** — gratis mekling (forbrukerradet.no)
3. **Forbrukerklageutvalget** — bindende avgjørelse, kostnad ~1 rettsgebyr (~860 NOK)
4. **Forliksrådet** — siste instans for småkrav

## Pricing Trap — «Rabattert» vs. Ordinær Pris

When the seller offers a replacement at a "discounted" or "rabattert" price:

1. **Verify the claimed "rabattert pris"** against the seller's own public web shop before accepting
2. **Document the regular price** — take a screenshot or note the URL and date
3. A "rabattert" price that is **higher than ordinary retail price** is a strong bad-faith indicator that should be raised explicitly in the formal complaint
4. **Argue**: If the seller offers a replacement model at a price *above* their own public list price, this is not a genuine attempt to resolve the complaint — it undermines their credibility and supports escalation

**Example from practice**: Fjellsport offered a "rabattert pris" of NOK 5.250 for a Polar Vantage M3, while the same product was listed at NOK 4.199 (ordinary price, no campaign) on fjellsport.no — a difference of NOK 1.051 above regular price.

## Product Cannot Be Repaired — Consequences

If the manufacturer (e.g., Polar) confirms the product **cannot be repaired**:

1. The seller cannot argue the consumer should accept repair — that option is off the table
2. The seller must provide **omlevering** (replacement with equivalent product) or **heving/prisavslag** (refund/price reduction) under forbrukerkjøpsloven §29
3. A discount code for 50% off a new product is not sufficient — the consumer is entitled to a remedy under the law, not a goodwill gesture
4. The manufacturer's internal warranty policy does **not** replace the seller's statutory obligations

## Ny reklamasjonsfrist ved omlevering

When a product is replaced (omlevering), a **new 2/5-year reclamation period** starts from the date of the replacement delivery. The consumer is not stuck with the original purchase date. Reference: forbrukerkjøpsloven §29, and Forbrukertilsynets guidance on omlevering.

## Ny reklamasjonsfrist ved reparasjon

When a product is repaired, the reclamation period for the repaired part is extended — the consumer gets at least the remainder of the original period, but in practice often a new 2-year period from the repair date for the repaired defect. Reference: forbrukerkjøpsloven §29 fjerde ledd.
