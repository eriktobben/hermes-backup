# Norwegian Consumer Law — Reklamasjon (Forbrukerkjøpsloven §27)

## Core Statute

**Forbrukerkjøpsloven §27** (lovdata.no/lov/2002-06-21-34):

> *"Reklamasjon må senest skje to år etter at tingen ble levert. Dersom tingen eller deler av den ved vanlig bruk er ment å vare vesentlig lenger, er fristen for å reklamere fem år."*

Translation: Complaint must be made within 2 years of delivery. If the item or parts of it, through normal use, are meant to last significantly longer, the deadline is 5 years.

## Key Arguments for 5-Year Period on Integrated Batteries

When a product has a **non-replaceable (integrated) battery** and the battery fails:

1. **Battery is an integral part** — cannot be separated from the product. When the battery fails, the entire product is unusable.
2. **Lovens ordlyd**: "tingen **eller deler av den**" — if the part (battery) can't be replaced independently, the product's total lifespan governs the reclamation period.
3. **Forventet levetid**: A premium sports watch (Polar Vantage V2, ~3500-4500 NOK) should reasonably last >3.5 years. Lithium-ion batteries in premium devices handle 500-800 charge cycles.
4. **Bevisbyrde**: The seller must prove the expected lifespan is ≤2 years, not the consumer the opposite.

## Counterargument to "Batteri har 2 års reklamasjon"

The seller may claim a distinction between hardware/software (5 years) and battery (2 years). **Rebuttal:**
- No statutory basis exists for this distinction
- If the battery is non-replaceable, the distinction is artificial — the product fails as a whole
- Forbrukerklageutvalget has ruled in multiple cases that integrated batteries in premium electronics fall under the 5-year rule

## Escalation Path (Norway)

1. **Skriftlig krav til selger** — henvis til §27, krev retting/omlevering
2. **Forbrukerrådet** — gratis mekling (forbrukerradet.no)
3. **Forbrukerklageutvalget** — bindende avgjørelse, kostnad ~1 rettsgebyr (~860 NOK)
4. **Forliksrådet** — siste instans for småkrav
